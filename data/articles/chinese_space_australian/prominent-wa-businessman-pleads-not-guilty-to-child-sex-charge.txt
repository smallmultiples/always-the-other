A prominent West Australian businessman who was heavily involved in Australian-Chinese business relations has pleaded not guilty to a charge of procuring a teenage girl to do an indecent act.Duncan Harvey Calder was charged last year over the alleged offence, which dates back to January 2008 and involves a girl who was then 14.He pleaded not guilty in the Perth Magistrates Court this morning and was released on bail until he appears in the District Court in April.Mr Calder resigned as national president of the Australia China Business Council after he was charged in June last year.Two months earlier, he had been part of a delegation which accompanied Prime Minister Tony Abbott on a trade mission to China.
